BRAZILIAN JIU-JITSU

If you have never heard about Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ), this section will let you know you some important facts around BJJ.

OUR HISTORY

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) was developed from Ju-jutsu, a Japanese martial art, by the Gracies. Since 1917, there are many efforts of the Gracies, especially Carlos & Helio Gracie, has been spent to make BJJ the most effective martial art in the world.

At the very beginning, BJJ showed it effectiveness by the dominance of Royce Gracie at Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC). He has fought successfully against many larger opponents in other fighting styles, and won 1st, 2nd, & 4th UFC.

Since then, BJJ has become a must-learn for MMA fighters, and also for people who interested in self-defense martial arts.

STYLE OF FIGHTING

Brazilian jiu-jitsu focuses on getting an opponent to the ground in order to neutralize possible strength or size advantages through ground fighting techniques and submission holds involving joint-locks and chokeholds. On the ground, physical strength can be offset or enhanced through proper grappling techniques.

BJJ employs a wide range of takedown techniques to bring an opponent to the ground such as "pulling guard", which is not used in other combat sports such as judo or wrestling. Once the opponent is on the ground, a number of manoeuvres (and counter-manoeuvres) are available to manipulate the opponent into a suitable position for the application of a submission technique.
